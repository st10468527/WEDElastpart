Mbali Mbele

Originally from Johannesburg, Mbali qualified from the University of KwaZulu Natal in 2020, and initially worked in small animal practices in kwaZulu Natal, before returning to Ireland to work in mixed practice. After setting up practice with the other doctors mentioned, she gained the Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons Certificate in Radiology in 2024 and is currently one of only a handful of vets in the country to have this qualification. Her current interests lie mainly in ultrasound radiology and endoscopy, in which she takes referral cases from other practices. .

Mbali enjoys karate in his free time as well as professionally spinning cars

Matshipi kgoadi

Matshipi is from Johannesburg and qualified as a vet from the University of Stellenbotch in 2018. She started her career in a busy small animal practice in Cape Town, SA. After almost two years there she returned home with her cat and two dogs to continue her career here in Johannesburg, joining the team in February 2021. Matshipi is interested in all aspects of veterinary medicine but is particularly keen on soft tissue surgery. She is presently studying for a Graduate Certificate in Veterinary Behaviour. .

Outside of work Matshipi enjoys yoga, hiking, walking her dog and playing/partaking in netball.

Keabetsoe Mokoena

Keabetsoe grew up familiar with life as a vet, watching and learning from her mother in his mixed practice in Pretoria. Following graduation from the University of Witwatersrand in 2017, she moved to the West where she worked in both referral and general practice before moving to Johannesburg. In January 2021 Keabetsoe joined the team. She has a particular interest in feline medicine, soft tissue surgery,diagnostic imaging and dentistry. .

Outside of work she is an avid reader and writer,as well as a lover of mix martial arts .
